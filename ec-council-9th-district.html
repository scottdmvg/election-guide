Rep. Cherelle Parker currently represents Northwest Philadelphia’s 200th legislative district in the PA House of Representatives. Elected in a 2005 special election as the youngest African-American woman to serve in the PA legislature, Parker currently chairs the House’s Philadelphia delegation. In Harrisburg, she has pushed for education funding and property-tax relief, and has fought against illegal guns, payday-lending abuse and voter-ID legislation. Parker, who served as a key aide to 9th District City Councilwoman Marian Tasco and a high-school teacher in Pleasantville, N.J., before her election to the House, is a graduate of the Philadelphia School District and Lincoln University (B.S., English education, 1994). A wife and mother, she lives in Mt. Airy.

Kevin Strickland grew up in Groton, CT, but his parents are both native Philadelphians who returned often when he was a child. Strickland also received his higher education here: a bachelor’s degree in political science from Temple and a master’s in professional and business communication from LaSalle. Currently the director of client relations for an emergency-medical travel-assistance company, Strickland lives in West Oak Lane with his wife and two daughters. He’s the GOP ward leader in the 10th Ward and served as deputy campaign manager for 2014 Republican congressional candidate Armond James.
